Diagnosed Errors from Q1–Q4
⚠ Error #1 — Interest Rate Anchor
Applied interest rate to revenue or EBITDA instead of the debt balance.
FIX: Interest = Interest Rate × Debt Balance (always). The % is a rate, the base is debt.
⚠ Error #2 — Management Dilution
Subtracted mgmt % from required equity instead of grossing up. mgmt dilution must inflate the total equity pool, not reduce what you need.
FIX: Total Equity = PE Proceeds Required ÷ (1 − Mgmt %)
⚠ Error #3 — Zero vs. Negative Taxes
Applied tax rate when EBT was zero or negative, generating phantom tax benefits that distort net income.
FIX: If EBT ≤ 0, taxes = $0. Tax shield is not modeled in paper LBOs.
⚠ Error #4 — Extra Forward Year Column
Added a full column for the forward year when it only needs EBITDA for exit multiple purposes. All other line items stop at hold period end.
FIX: Count hold years exactly. Add one forward-only column with EBITDA only.
⚠ Error #5 — Stopped One Step Short (Q4)
Calculated MoM correctly (3.18x) but didn't solve back to the capex budget. Got the intermediate answer, missed the actual ask.
FIX: On solve-back questions always re-read the ask after getting MoM. What is the question actually asking for?
⚠ Error #6 — Revenue vs. EBITDA for Debt
Multiplied leverage multiple by revenue ($100) instead of EBITDA ($20), getting $500 debt instead of $100.
FIX: Leverage multiple is always × EBITDA. Write "EBITDA × Lev = Debt" before starting.
Pattern Summary
Root Cause Analysis
1
Structure is solid — mechanics are slipping
You understand the flow (assumptions → entry → IS → CF → exit). Errors are downstream calculation mistakes, not conceptual gaps. This is fixable fast.
2
Base confusion is the #1 issue
Interest anchored to wrong base. Debt anchored to wrong base. Margin anchored to wrong base. Drill: Interest = rate × debt, Debt = lev × EBITDA, D&A = % × revenue, EBITDA = margin × revenue.
3
Solve-backwards questions need a final inference step
Q1 (exit multiple) and Q4 (capex budget) require solving backwards from a constraint. Always ask: "what is the binding constraint?" then work backwards to zero.

Core Formulas
Entry Mechanics
Entry TEV = EBITDA × Entry Multiple
Initial Debt = EBITDA × Leverage Multiple
Entry Equity = Entry TEV − Initial Debt
⚠ Leverage is ALWAYS × EBITDA, never × revenue. Write this first before anything else.
Income Statement Stack
Revenue
× EBITDA Margin = EBITDA
− D&A (% of Revenue) = EBIT
− Interest (rate × Debt Balance) = EBT
− Taxes (rate × EBT, floor $0) = Net Income
D&A is % of revenue, not EBITDA. Interest base is debt balance, not revenue or EBITDA.
Free Cash Flow
FCF = Net Income
+ D&A
− CapEx
− ΔWorking Capital
NWC use is always NEGATIVE in the CF statement (cash goes out to fund working capital). An increase in NWC = cash outflow.
Exit & Returns
Exit TEV = Exit EBITDA × Exit Multiple
Exit Equity = Exit TEV − Ending Net Debt
MoM = Exit Equity ÷ Entry Equity
IRR ≈ Rule of 72 / 114 / 144 ÷ Years
Exit EBITDA = forward year (year after hold end). Ending Net Debt = Initial Debt − Cumulative CF (if paying down annually) or Initial Debt − total CF at exit (lump sum).
Management Dilution (Gross-Up)
PE needs $X at exit
Mgmt owns Y%
Total Exit Equity = X ÷ (1 − Y%)
Never subtract Y% from X. Gross up. Then add ending debt to get exit TEV. Then divide by exit EBITDA for exit multiple.
Solve-Backwards Template
Target MoM → Required Exit Equity
+ Ending Net Debt → Required Exit TEV
÷ Exit EBITDA → Required Exit Multiple
For capex budget: Required Exit Equity → Required Ending Debt → Required CF paydown → Slack vs. actual CF generated = max capex.

Process A — Simple Multiple (No IS)
1
Anchor EBITDA
Identify entry EBITDA. Write it down first. This is the base for everything.
2
Entry TEV & Debt
TEV = EBITDA × Entry Multiple
Debt = EBITDA × Leverage Multiple
3
Entry Equity
Equity = TEV − Debt
4
Ending Debt
Apply given paydown % to initial debt. Or use cumulative CF if specified.
5
Target Exit Equity (Gross Up for Mgmt if Needed)
Target Equity = Entry Equity × MoM
If mgmt dilution: Total Equity = Target ÷ (1 − Mgmt%)
6
Exit TEV → Exit Multiple
Exit TEV = Exit Equity + Ending Debt
Exit Multiple = Exit TEV ÷ Exit EBITDA
Process B — Full IS + CF Build
1
Write Assumptions Box First
Interest rate, tax rate, capex %, D&A %, NWC rule, leverage, entry & exit multiple.
2
Build Entry
Entry EBITDA = Year 1 Revenue × Margin
Debt = Entry EBITDA × Leverage
TEV = EBITDA × Entry Multiple
Equity = TEV − Debt
3
Build Revenue Schedule
Year by year. Hold period + 1 forward year (EBITDA only in forward year).
4
Income Statement (each year)
Revenue → EBITDA → (−D&A) → EBIT → (−Interest on beg. debt balance) → EBT → (−Tax, floor $0) → Net Income
5
Cash Flow (each year)
CF = NI + D&A − CapEx − ΔNWC
NWC outflow is negative. CapEx is negative.
6
Debt Schedule (if annual paydown)
Beg Debt = Prior Ending Debt
End Debt = Beg Debt − CF (floor $0)
Next year's interest uses new beg balance.
7
Exit
Exit TEV = Forward EBITDA × Exit Multiple
Exit Equity = Exit TEV − Ending Net Debt
8
Returns
MoM = Exit Equity ÷ Entry Equity
IRR ≈ Rule of 72/114/144 ÷ Hold Years
Common Traps Checklist
✓
Did I use EBITDA (not revenue) as the leverage base?
Debt = Leverage Multiple × EBITDA. Never revenue.
✓
Did I apply interest to the right debt balance?
If annual paydown: interest on beginning-of-year balance. If lump sum: interest on original debt throughout.
✓
Did I floor taxes at $0?
If EBT ≤ 0, taxes = $0. Net Income = EBT when EBT is negative (no tax benefit modeled).
✓
Is NWC a subtraction in my CF?
Increase in NWC = cash use = negative in FCF. Always subtract ΔNWC.
✓
Did I use the forward year EBITDA for exit (not the last hold year)?
Exit multiple applies to the year AFTER the hold ends. Build that column with EBITDA only.
✓
On solve-back questions: did I answer the actual ask?
Getting MoM is not always the final step. Re-read the question after computing returns.
New Practice Problems — Targeting Your Weak Points
Problem 5 — Management Dilution + Simple Exit
Difficulty: 1 / 3

You acquire Company PQR with the following characteristics:

  • Annual EBITDA of $20mm with no growth. Entry multiple is 6x EBITDA.
  • Initial net leverage is 4x EBITDA. Interest rate is 8%.
  • At exit, debt is paid down 40% from cumulative cash flow. No income statement needed — assume cash generated equals $32mm cumulatively over the hold.
  • Management owns 25% of total equity through awarded options.
What EBITDA multiple must you sell Company PQR at to generate a 2.5x MoM for the PE investor? Assume no EBITDA growth.
Step-by-Step Solution
  • Entry EBITDA = $20mm. Entry TEV = $20 × 6x = $120mm
  • Initial Debt = $20 × 4x = $80mm. Entry Equity = $120 − $80 = $40mm
  • PE needs 2.5x MoM → Required PE proceeds = $40 × 2.5 = $100mm
  • GROSS UP for mgmt 25%: Total Exit Equity = $100 ÷ (1 − 0.25) = $100 ÷ 0.75 = $133.3mm
  • Ending Net Debt = $80 − $32 cumulative CF = $48mm (40% paydown = $32, confirms: $80 × 0.40 = $32 ✓)
  • Exit TEV = $133.3 + $48 = $181.3mm
  • Exit Multiple = $181.3 ÷ $20 = ~9.1x
Watch For
The trap is using $100 as the exit equity instead of $133.3. Mgmt's 25% comes out of the total pool — you must gross up, not subtract. If you got ~7.6x you made that error.
Problem 6 — Full IS Build, Annual Debt Paydown
Difficulty: 2 / 3

You perform an LBO on Company MNO at the end of 2022.

  • Company MNO will have revenues of $150mm in 2023, growing by $15mm annually. EBITDA margin is 25%. D&A is 8% of revenue. CapEx is $10mm per year.
  • No change in net working capital. Tax rate is 40%. Initial leverage is 4x forward EBITDA at an interest rate of 8%.
  • Cash flow pays down debt at the end of each year. Interest calculated on beginning debt balance.
  • Sell the company at the end of 3 years at 10x forward EBITDA.
What is the implied MoM and approximate IRR?
Step-by-Step Solution
  • Entry EBITDA (2023E) = $150 × 25% = $37.5mm. Initial Debt = $37.5 × 4x = $150mm
  • Entry TEV = $37.5 × 10x = $375mm. Entry Equity = $375 − $150 = $225mm
  • 2023: Rev $150, EBITDA $37.5, D&A ($12), EBIT $25.5, Interest ($12 = 8%×$150), EBT $13.5, Tax ($5.4), NI $8.1, +D&A $12, −CapEx ($10), CF = $10.1mm. End Debt = $150 − $10.1 = $139.9mm
  • 2024: Rev $165, EBITDA $41.25, D&A ($13.2), EBIT $28.05, Interest ($11.2 = 8%×$139.9), EBT $16.85, Tax ($6.7), NI $10.15, +D&A $13.2, −CapEx ($10), CF = $13.35mm. End Debt = $126.6mm
  • 2025: Rev $180, EBITDA $45, D&A ($14.4), EBIT $30.6, Interest ($10.1 = 8%×$126.6), EBT $20.5, Tax ($8.2), NI $12.3, +D&A $14.4, −CapEx ($10), CF = $16.7mm. End Debt = $109.9mm
  • Forward EBITDA (2026E): Rev $195, EBITDA = $195 × 25% = $48.75mm
  • Exit TEV = $48.75 × 10x = $487.5mm. Exit Equity = $487.5 − $109.9 = $377.6mm
  • MoM = $377.6 ÷ $225 = ~1.7x. IRR: 1.7x over 3 yrs → below 2x, use Rule of 72 as ceiling → below 24%. Approx ~19%
Watch For
1) Interest must use beginning debt balance each year — it decreases as you pay down. 2) D&A is 8% of revenue ($150/$165/$180), not of EBITDA. 3) 2026 is forward year — EBITDA only, no IS below it.
Problem 7 — Solve Backwards for Max CapEx
Difficulty: 2 / 3

Your PE firm acquires Company STU at the end of 2021.

  • Company STU will have revenues of $80mm in 2022, growing $10mm annually. EBITDA margin is 30%. D&A is 5% of revenue. No change in NWC. Tax rate is 50%.
  • Initial leverage is 5x forward EBITDA at 10% interest. No debt is paid down during the hold — all cash accumulates and is used at exit.
  • Entry and exit multiple is 8x forward EBITDA. Sell at end of 4 years.
How much can you cumulatively spend on capital expenditures over the hold period while still achieving a 3x MoM?
Step-by-Step Solution
  • Entry EBITDA (2022E) = $80 × 30% = $24mm. Debt = $24 × 5x = $120mm. TEV = $24 × 8x = $192mm. Equity = $72mm
  • Target exit equity for 3x MoM = $72 × 3 = $216mm
  • Forward EBITDA (2026E): Rev = $80 + ($10×4) = $120mm in 2025, forward year $130mm. EBITDA = $130 × 30% = $39mm
  • Exit TEV = $39 × 8x = $312mm
  • Required Ending Debt = Exit TEV − Required Exit Equity = $312 − $216 = $96mm
  • Max Debt Paydown Allowed = $120 − $96 = $24mm
  • Now build CF without CapEx: Interest = 10% × $120 = $12/yr (fixed, no paydown). IS each year → NI + D&A = pre-CapEx CF. Cumulative ≈ 2022: NI $6+D&A $4=$10 | 2023: $7.5+$4.5=$12 | 2024: $9+$5=$14 | 2025: $10.5+$5.5=$16 → Total pre-CapEx CF = ~$52mm
  • Max CapEx = Pre-CapEx CF − Required Paydown = $52 − $24 = ~$28mm
Watch For
The key insight: solve backwards from the 3x constraint to find required ending debt first. Then compare to what cash you actually generate. The gap is your CapEx budget. Don't try to solve forward with CapEx as X — work backwards.
Problem 8 — Margin Expansion + Annual Paydown (Hard)
Difficulty: 3 / 3

You perform an LBO on Company VWX at the end of 2020.

  • Revenues of $50mm in 2021, growing 20% annually. EBITDA margin starts at 15% and expands by 500bps every 2 years (15% in 2021–2022, 20% in 2023–2024, 25% in 2025–2026).
  • D&A is 10% of revenue. CapEx is 8% of revenue. NWC increases $3mm per year (use of cash). Tax rate is 50%.
  • Initial leverage is 4x forward EBITDA at 12% interest. Debt paid down with available CF at end of each year. Interest on beginning balance.
  • Sell at end of 4 years at 10x forward EBITDA.
What is the implied MoM and approximate IRR?
Step-by-Step Solution
  • Entry EBITDA (2021E) = $50 × 15% = $7.5mm. Debt = $7.5 × 4x = $30mm. TEV = $7.5 × 10x = $75mm. Equity = $45mm
  • Revenue: 2021=$50, 2022=$60, 2023=$72, 2024=$86.4, fwd 2025=$103.7
  • Margins: 2021–22 = 15%, 2023–24 = 20%, fwd 2025 = 25%
  • 2021: EBITDA $7.5, D&A ($5), EBIT $2.5, Int ($3.6=12%×$30), EBT ($1.1) → Tax $0, NI ($1.1), +D&A $5, −CapEx ($4=8%×$50), −NWC ($3), CF = ($3.1mm). Can't pay down — End Debt stays $30mm
  • 2022: EBITDA $9, D&A ($6), EBIT $3, Int ($3.6=12%×$30), EBT ($0.6) → Tax $0, NI ($0.6), +D&A $6, −CapEx ($4.8), −NWC ($3), CF = ($2.4mm). End Debt stays $30mm
  • 2023: EBITDA $14.4, D&A ($7.2), EBIT $7.2, Int ($3.6), EBT $3.6, Tax ($1.8), NI $1.8, +D&A $7.2, −CapEx ($5.76), −NWC ($3), CF = $0.24mm. End Debt = $29.76mm
  • 2024: EBITDA $17.3, D&A ($8.6), EBIT $8.7, Int ($3.57=12%×$29.76), EBT $5.1, Tax ($2.6), NI $2.5, +D&A $8.6, −CapEx ($6.9), −NWC ($3), CF = $1.2mm. End Debt = $28.6mm
  • Fwd EBITDA (2025E) = $103.7 × 25% = $25.9mm. Exit TEV = $25.9 × 10x = $259mm
  • Exit Equity = $259 − $28.6 = $230.4mm. MoM = $230.4 ÷ $45 = ~5.1x. IRR: 5x over 4 years → Rule of 144 gives ~36% as 4x anchor, slightly above → approx ~50%+ (high because margin expansion is massive)
Watch For
1) Years with negative CF cannot pay down debt — floor debt paydown at $0. 2) NWC outflow of $3mm is subtracted every year without fail. 3) Margin expansion is the value driver here — the exit EBITDA of $25.9mm is 3.5x the entry EBITDA of $7.5mm even though revenue "only" doubled.
